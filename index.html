Got more questions?

What is KYO? +
KYO (Know Your Opportunities) is a structured quiz powered by AI and psychometric models. It evaluates a student’s profile based on key parameters that define international student success. By identifying gap areas, KYO enables our counsellors and students to develop personalised action plans and make informed decisions regarding course selection and scholarship opportunities.
How do I know which plan is right for me? +
The right plan depends on your goals, background, and the level of support you require. Our programmes cater to three types of applicants:

• Seeker – Ideal for students with prior international experience, such as studying or working abroad, who feel confident navigating academic and cultural transitions. It is also suited for those with strong personal support systems, such as family guidance. This plan provides essential admission assistance, basic scholarship information, university application and visa guidance, and comprehensive pre-departure support.

• Scholar – Best for students studying abroad for the first time who want structured preparation for academic success and adaptation to new teaching and assessment styles. This plan is highly recommended for those aiming for scholarships and career advancement, offering advanced scholarship guidance, assured scholarship opportunities, strategic profile building, summer planning, AI-driven academic success strategies, and IELTS practice.

• Scholar Plus – Students are interviewed and access to this programme is only upon selection. It Includes all Scholar plan benefits, with additional hands-on support for scholarship applications and profile enhancement. Our team prepares and submits scholarship applications on your behalf. This plan is ideal for students seeking top-tier universities with complex application processes or those targeting countries offering full or partial tuition waivers. Scholar Plus also provides university-specific mentoring (guidance from current or former students of your chosen university) and exclusive international internship opportunities to strengthen career prospects.
Do these plans cover all countries and courses? +
Courses or countries requiring extensive documentation and strategic planning fall under the Scholar Plus plan. Standard application processes, including those for many globally ranked top 1% universities, are supported across all three plans.
Is IELTS required to get a scholarship? +
It depends on the university and country’s visa requirements. Some institutions waive IELTS based on academic performance or alternative language proficiency tests.
Can I get a guaranteed scholarship under the Scholar or Scholar Plus plan? +
We ensure the strongest possible application, but scholarship decisions rest with the issuing authority (universities, governments, or NGOs). However, since we carefully select our scholars, if you do not receive any scholarship offers, we refund the principal amount (excluding taxes).
What kind of scholarships will I receive under each plan? +
• Seeker – Access to all profile asked relevant scholarship information for self-application.

• Scholar – Information and Guidance on merit- and need-based scholarships, as well as financial aid options. At least one scholarship is assured.

• Scholar Plus – Maximum funding opportunities, including applications for partial or full tuition waivers. We handle all applications on your behalf. At least one scholarship is assured.
Can I upgrade from one plan to another? +
Yes, you can upgrade by paying the difference in plan costs.
Which plan is best for the world top 100 or Ivy League or Fully sponsored universities? +

Scholar Plus is ideal for that. However, the access to this programme is via a selection interview.

Can I receive only scholarship guidance without other services? +

Yes, the Scholar plan is specifically designed for scholarship guidance. You will receive the access to other modules on your LMS but you can choose not to utilise them.

What is Strategic Summer Planning, and how does it help students? +

Strategic Summer Planning helps students utilise their academic breaks effectively by engaging in relevant projects, courses, skill development, and internships. This strengthens university applications and enhances overall career opportunities.
